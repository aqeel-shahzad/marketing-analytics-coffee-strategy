# Marketing Analytics: Private-Label Coffee Strategy

## Project Overview

This project uses marketing analytics to design and justify a new private-label coffee product for a UK-based grocery retailer. The analysis combines customer segmentation, conjoint analysis, perceptual positioning, dashboarding, and marketing strategy to identify a commercially attractive product concept and target customer segment.

The final recommendation is a **sustainable dark-roast 100% Arabica capsule coffee product priced at £5**, supported by customer data analysis, product preference modelling, competitor positioning, and Power BI dashboard insights.

## Project Highlights

* Analysed customer purchasing and behavioural data to identify attractive target segments.
* Used segmentation analysis to support customer targeting and marketing strategy.
* Applied conjoint analysis to evaluate customer preferences across product attributes.
* Used PCA positioning to compare the proposed product against competing coffee products.
* Built a Power BI dashboard to communicate product, pricing, targeting, and positioning evidence.
* Developed a full 4P marketing mix strategy covering product, price, place, and promotion.
* Translated customer and market analytics into a clear private-label product recommendation.

## Business Problem

A UK-based grocery retailer wants to launch a new private-label coffee product. The challenge is to identify an attractive customer segment, understand customer preferences, position the new product against competitors, and design a marketing strategy that aligns with customer needs and retailer objectives.

The goal of this project is to determine which customer segment and coffee product concept the retailer should target, using analytical evidence rather than assumptions.

## Objectives

* Analyse customer and transaction data to identify attractive target segments.
* Understand coffee product preferences using conjoint analysis.
* Evaluate product positioning against competing coffee products.
* Design a private-label coffee product concept aligned with customer needs.
* Build a Power BI dashboard to support marketing decision-making.
* Translate analytical findings into a practical 4P marketing strategy.

## Tools, Methods, and Analytical Approach

* **R:** data preparation, segmentation analysis, conjoint analysis, PCA, and visualisation.
* **Power BI:** dashboard design, KPI cards, slicers, and business storytelling.
* **Excel:** data review, validation, and supporting analysis.
* **Customer segmentation:** identification of attractive customer groups for targeting.
* **Conjoint analysis:** evaluation of customer preferences across coffee product attributes.
* **PCA positioning:** comparison of the proposed product against competitor offerings.
* **Marketing mix analysis:** development of product, price, place, and promotion strategy.
* **Dashboarding:** communication of customer, product, and pricing insights for decision-makers.

## Key Findings

* The analysis supported targeting customers who value convenience, quality, and sustainability.
* Capsule format was selected because it aligns with convenience-led coffee consumption and provides clear product differentiation.
* Dark roast 100% Arabica was selected to support a premium quality perception.
* Sustainability was included as a product attribute to strengthen positioning and appeal to conscious consumers.
* The £5 price point balances premium perception with private-label value expectations.
* Dashboard insights supported the final product, pricing, and targeting decisions.

## Analytical Outcomes and Decision Value

* Designed a data-supported private-label coffee product concept for a UK grocery retailer.
* Connected customer segmentation, conjoint analysis, and positioning outputs to practical marketing decisions.
* Used dashboarding to communicate evidence behind target segment selection, product format, pricing, and positioning.
* Translated analytical outputs into a coherent 4P marketing strategy.
* Demonstrated how marketing analytics can support product development, retail strategy, and customer targeting decisions.

## Business Recommendations

**Launch a sustainable dark-roast 100% Arabica capsule coffee product.**
The proposed product combines quality, convenience, and sustainability while remaining suitable for a private-label grocery strategy.

**Target customers who value convenience, quality, and ethical product cues.**
The product should be positioned toward shoppers who are open to premium-style coffee but still expect strong value from a grocery retailer’s private-label range.

**Use a £5 price point to balance premium positioning and accessibility.**
The selected price supports a quality-led product image while remaining competitive within the private-label category.

**Promote the product around quality, sustainability, and everyday convenience.**
Marketing messages should highlight dark roast flavour, 100% Arabica quality, sustainable credentials, and capsule convenience.

**Use dashboard insights to support future product and campaign decisions.**
The Power BI dashboard can help stakeholders understand the customer rationale, product positioning, and pricing logic behind the recommendation.
